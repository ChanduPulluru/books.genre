Horror

The Hunger

-Alma Katsu

The Hunger is a book about the ill-fated Donner Party journey in 1846. The book is a blend of historical fiction and horror, and weaves a tale of desperation, survival, and the supernatural. The travelers are driven to the brink of madness by depleted rations, bitter quarrels, and the mysterious death of a little boy.

The Shining

-Stephen King

“The Shining” by Stephen King is a psychological horror novel12. It tells the story of the Torrance family, who confront their inner demons while staying at the isolated Overlook Hotel during the winter season. The novel explores themes of addiction, supernatural forces, and the destructive power of isolation

Flowers in the Attic

-V.C.Andrews

Flowers in the Attic is a Gothic novel by V. C. Andrews. It is the first book in the Dollanganger series, which includes titles like Petals on the Wind, If There Be Thorns, and Seeds of Yesterday. The story revolves around the Dollanganger children and their secrets, set in a suspenseful and thrilling tale of family, greed, murder, and forbidden love.

Thriller

The Other Black Girl

-Zakiya Dalila Harris

The Other Black Girl is a debut novel by Zakiya Dalila Harris that combines social satire and thriller elements. The novel follows Nella Rogers, a Black editorial assistant at a white-dominated publishing house, who faces tension and threats from another Black woman, Hazel, who is part of a conspiracy to sabotage Black women in the workplace.

The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo

-Stieg Larsson

The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo is a gripping thriller that follows investigative journalist Mikael Blomkvist and hacker Lisbeth Salander as they delve into the mysterious disappearance of a wealthy industrialist’s niece with a spellbinding amalgam of murder mystery,family saga,love story,and financial intrigue.

Gone Girl

-Gillian Flynn

Gone Girl by Gillian Flynn is a psychological thriller that delves into the dark secrets of a troubled marriage. When Amy Dunne disappears on her fifth wedding anniversary, her husband Nick becomes the prime suspect. The novel is filled with plot twists that subvert reader expectations.

Mystery

A Time to Kill

-John Grisham

The life of a ten-year-old black girl is shattered by two drunken and remorseless white men. The mostly white town of Clanton in Ford County, Mississippi, reacts with shock and horror at the inhuman crime—until the girl's father acquires an assault rifle and takes justice into his own hands.

In Cold Blood

-Truman Capote

In Cold Blood details the events leading up to, including, and following the November 15, 1959 murders of Herbert Clutter, his wife, and two gof their four children on their farm in Holcomb, Kansas.In Cold Blood explores the lives of those affected by and involved in the murders.

Sherlock Holmes

-Sir Arthur Conan Doyle

The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes is a collection of twelve short stories by Arthur Conan Doyle. Published in 1892, the stories involve the peculiar exploits of the sharp-witted detective Sherlock Holmes, whose fierce energy and acute observation and reasoning skills enable him to solve the strangest of crimes.

Historical

The Book Thief

-Markus Zusak

The Book Thief is a book about a girl during World War II in Nazi Germany. Her foster parents hide a Jewish man, but this man is forced to leave once attention is drawn to the family. Ultimately the girls' town is bombed, and her foster parents and best friend are killed.

War and Peace

-Leo Tolstoy

The main focus of War and Peace is the invasion of Russia by Napoleon in 1812. It follows three well-known characters within literature: Natasha Rostov, Prince Andrei Bolkonsky, and Pierre Bezukhoy. The novel follows these three different stories as they intersect in this chaotic time.

Gone With The Wind

-Margaret Mitchell

Gone with the Wind offers a romanticized view of the American Civil War from the perspective of the Confederacy. It focuses on Scarlett O'Hara, the beautiful daughter of a wealthy planter, following her romantic trials during the war and into the Reconstruction period.

Fantasy

Harry Potter

-J.K.Rowling

Harry Potter is an 11-year-old orphan living with his cruel aunt and uncle. On his eleventh birthday, he discovers that he is a wizard and is soon admitted to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. The novel follows Harry as he learns about his past, makes lifelong friends, and faces the mysteries surrounding the Sorcerer’s Stone.

The Lord of the Rings

-J.R.R. Tolkien

The Lord of the Rings is an epic fantasy novel by J.R.R. Tolkien. It tells the story of a group of hobbits, elves, humans, and wizards who set out on a quest to destroy an evil ring before it can be used by an evil being called Sauron to enslave all of Middle-Earth.The book is about power and greed, innocence and enlightenment.

A Hero Born

-Jin Yong

A Hero Born is a tale of fantasy and wonder, love and passion, treachery and war, betrayal and brotherhood. And then a hero is born... After his father, a Song patriot, was murdered, Guo Jing and his mother fled to the plains and joins Genghis Khan and his people.

Sci-Fic

Dune

-Frank Herbert

It tells the story of young Paul Atreides, whose family accepts the stewardship of the planet Arrakis. While the planet is an inhospitable and sparsely populated desert wasteland, it is the only source of melange, or "spice", a drug that extends life and enhances mental abilities.

A Wrinkle In Time

-Madeleine L’engle

A Wrinkle in Time tells the story of 13-year-old Meg, who is frustrated with how she fits in at school and distracted by the long absence of her father. Meg sets off on an interstellar journey to rescue her father, accompanied by her precocious young brother Charles Wallace and her new friend Calvin.

The Time Machine

-H.G.Wells

The Time Machine, published in 1895 by British author H. G. Wells, is a science fiction novella that follows an unnamed narrator as he recounts the story of a time traveler who journeys to the year 802,701 AD. There, he encounters the Eloi and Morlocks, two divergent human species.

Romance

Twilight

-Stephenie Meyer

Twilight is a captivating novel by Stephenie Meyer. Set in the small town of Forks, it tells the story of Bella, a teenager who falls in love with a vampire named Edward. Their unconventional relationship unfolds in a world where love and danger collide.

Romeo And Juliet

-William Shakespeare

Shakespeare’s Romeo and Juliet tells the tale of a young man and woman, who fall in love but are destined for tragedy due to their warring families - the Montagues and the Capulets.

Safe Haven

-Nicholas Sparks

Nicholas Sparks's novel Safe Haven (2010) is a story of hope and love prevailing against abuse and distress. With a fake identity and very little money, a mysterious young woman named Katie arrives in Southport, North Carolina, leaving behind a life engulfed in torture and horror.

Westerns

True Grit

-Charles Portis

The novel follows 14-year-old Mattie Ross as she seeks to avenge her father's murder with the help of the ruthless Marshal Rooster Cogburn. Filled with vivid characters and sharp dialogue, it is a timeless classic that explores themes of justice, courage, and the enduring power of grit.

The Gunslinger

-Stephen King

The book tells the story of The Gunslinger, Roland of Gilead, and his quest to catch the man in black, the first of many steps toward Roland's ultimate destination, The Dark Tower. The main story takes place in a world somewhat similar to the Old West but in an alternate timeframe or parallel universe.

Redeeming Love

-Francine Rivers

Redeeming Love by Francine Rivers is a powerful historical romance that tells the story of a woman named Angel who finds redemption and love through the unwavering devotion of a man named Michael. Set in 1850s California, this novel is a beautiful portrayal of God's unconditional love.

Autobiography

Long Walk To Freedom

-Nelson Mandela

"Long Walk to Freedom" is an autobiographical book by Nelson Mandela.Includes Mandela's life story, from his childhood to his development into a freedom fighter and his remarkable role in the construction of a new, democratic South Africa.

Wings Of Fire

-Dr.A P J Abdul Kalam

"Wings of Fire" is the autobiography of Dr. Abdul Kalam, former President of India. The book traces his journey from a humble South Indian boy to his role in developing India's nuclear weapons and eventually becoming president.

The Hiding Place

-Corrie Ten Boon

"The Hiding Place" is a memoir by Corrie ten Boom, a Dutch woman who, along with her family,helped hide Jews during the Nazi occupation of the Netherlands.It is a powerful story of faith,love,and sacrifice during World War II

Personality

Atomic Habits

-Jmaes Clear

Atomic Habits by James Clear is a practical guide to breaking bad behaviors and adopting good ones in four steps123. The book shows you how to focus on your systems, not your goals, and how small changes can compound into remarkable results.

Why Has Nobody Told Me This Befor?

-Dr.Julie Smith

'Why Has Nobody Told Me This Before?' by Julie Smith is a memoir that sheds light on women's experiences growing up in patriarchal societies. The book provides invaluable guidance for any woman seeking to empower herself and live a fulfilling life.

The Mountain Is You

-Brianna Wiest

“The Mountain is You” is a book by Brianna Wiest that serves as an introspective guide to confronting the barriers we erect for ourselves.The book delves deep into the psyche, unraveling the intricate patterns of self-sabotage,and offers tangible solutions to transcend them.

Inspirational

Think Like a Monk

-Jay Shetty

"Think Like a Monk" by Jay Shetty combines ancient wisdom and personal experiences to help readers overcome negative thoughts, access stillness, and create true purpose.The book encourages self-reflection and provides strategies for mental clarity, self-awareness, and resilience3. Shetty shares insights from his time as a monk and emphasizes the importance of living with purpose aligned with one's true self.

Rich Dad Poor Dad

-Robert T.Kiyosaki

Rich Dad Poor Dad contrasts the financial philosophies of two father figures in Robert Kiyosaki's life: his biological father (the 'Poor Dad') and his friend's father (the 'Rich Dad')12. The book emphasizes using money as a tool for wealth development, challenges the idea that the rich are born wealthy, and explains the difference between assets and liabilities.

Make Your Bed

-Admiral William H.McRaven

"Make Your Bed: Little Things That Can Change Your Life...And Maybe the World" by Admiral William H. McRaven is a motivational book based on his famous commencement speech at the University of Texas at Austin. The book outlines ten life lessons he learned from Navy SEAL training, which can help anyone overcome challenges and achieve success.

Health

The New Encyclopedia of Modern Bodybuilding

-Arnold Schwarzenegger

Arnold covers the very latest advances in both weight training and bodybuilding competition, with new sections on diet and nutrition, sports psychology, the treatment and prevention of injuries, and methods of training, each illustrated with detailed photos of some of bodybuilding's newest stars.

If Our Bodies Could Talk

-James Hamblin,M.D.

“If Our Bodies Could Talk” by James Hamblin, M.D., is an informative book that explores various aspects of human health and biology. Hamblin, a preventive medicine physician and senior editor at The Atlantic, uses humor to answer common and quirky questions about the human body.

The Mind Gut Connection

-Emeran Mayer

“The Mind-Gut Connection” by Dr. Emeran Mayer delves into the intricate relationship between the brain and the digestive system, highlighting the bi-directional communication through the gut-brain axis. The book emphasizes the crucial role of gut microbiota with accessible tips for overall well-being.

History

And There Was Light

-Jon Meacham

This book tells the story of Lincoln from his birth on the Kentucky frontier in 1809 to his leadership during the Civil War to his tragic assassination in 1865: his rise, his self-education, his loves, his bouts of depression, his political failures, his deepening faith, and his persistent conviction that slavery must end.

Salt:A World History

-Mark Kurlansky

Salt: A World History is a book that explores how salt has shaped human civilization through the centuries, causing wars and the rise and fall of empires. The book begins at the start of recorded history and highlights humanity’s dependence on salt, up to roughly present day times.

The Diary Of Young Girl

-Anne Frank

The Diary of a Young Girl by Anne Frank is a powerful memoir of a teenage girl living in hiding during the Nazi occupation of Amsterdam. Written by Anne Frank from 1942 to 1944, the diary reveals the humanity of those who suffered at the hands of the Nazis during their occupation of the Netherlands.

Bussiness

Thinking,Fast and Slow

-Daniel Kahneman

Thinking, Fast and Slow by psychologist Daniel Kahneman explains how two systems in your brain are constantly fighting over control of your behavior and actions, and teaches you the many ways in which this leads to errors in memory, judgment and decisions, and what you can do about it. The book's main thesis is a differentiation between two modes of thought: "System 1" is fast, instinctive and emotional; "System 2" is slower, more deliberative, and more logical.

Dare To Lead

-Brene Brown

Dare to Lead is a book by Brené Brown that dispels common myths about modern-day workplace culture and shows that true leadership requires vulnerability, values, trust, and resilience.It tells that a Brave leadership requires four skills: facing vulnerability with courage.

Good To Great

-James C.Collins

Good to Great by Jim Collins explores how companies transition from being ordinary to becoming great and outperforming their competitors. The book emphasizes that good is the enemy of great, and that conscious choice and discipline are key factors in achieving greatness. Collins shares insights from a five-year research project comparing companies that made the leap to greatness with those that did not.

The Law of Attraction

"The Law of Attraction" book explains how to use the fundamental law of attraction to manifest the life you desire. Prayer

-Timothy Keller

In his book "Prayer," Timothy Keller explores the purpose of prayer, emphasizing its transformative power. He explains that prayer is not just about getting what we want from God, but about aligning our desires with His will. Keller provides theological insights, practical guidance, and specific prayers for various situations.

Education

The Knowledge Gap

-Natalie Wexler

The Knowledge Gap is a book that examines what elementary literacy instruction currently looks like, how it evolved to this point, and how it can be changed so that all students receive a meaningful, rigorous education. The book follows the stories of educators, students, and parents.

How We Learn

-Benedict Carey

"How We Learn" is a book by Benedict Carey that explores the intersection of computer science, neurobiology, and cognitive psychology to explain how learning truly works. The author delves into decades of education research to uncover insights on how our brains absorb and retain information.

Why Don’t Students Like School?

-Daniel T. Willingham

The book "Why Don’t Students Like School?" by Daniel T. Willingham explores the reasons why students often struggle with learning and dislike school. It emphasizes the importance of motivation, engagement, and meaningful learning experiences, and provides practical strategies for teachers to make learning more effective and enjoyable.

Travel

Destinations of a Lifetime

-National Geographic

Destinations of a Lifetime by National Geographic takes you on a breathtaking journey to some of the most extraordinary places on Earth. Filled with stunning photography and insightful commentary, this book is a celebration of the world's diverse landscapes, cultures, and experiences.

Travels with Charley

-John Steinbeck

Travels with Charley: In Search of America is a 1962 travelogue written by American author John Steinbeck. It documents the driving trip he took with his poodle, Charley, around the United States in the 1960s. Steinbeck wrote that he was moved by a desire to see his country on a personal level, since he made his living writing about it.

Wild

-Cheryl Strayed

Wild by Cheryl Strayed is a memoir that chronicles the author's journey of self-discovery as she embarks on a 1,100-mile solo hike along the Pacific Crest Trail. Strayed set out on her journey at the age of 26, alone and with no prior hiking experience, seeking self-discovery and resolution of her enduring grief and personal challenges.
